THE VAMPYRE

From the Carpathian shadows, where life and death entwine.

November 8, 1115 DR Helen Voss

The Vampyre is a creature of unending twilight — born from corruption, bound by blood, and sustained through the essence of the living. Scholars of the occult have long debated its origin: some claim it was man’s punishment for defying death, others insist it is a race older than humanity itself, bred from darkness before the first dawn. By daylight, the Vampyre lies dormant, its body cold and unmoving, the semblance of death upon its features. Yet by night, it awakens with all the grace of its former life — eloquent, alluring, and terrible. The creature’s charm is its first weapon. It speaks softly, commands gently, and in its presence mortals feel a strange, unholy reverence, as though before a fallen angel.

Its sustenance is the blood of the living, taken not for pleasure but necessity. Blood is life, and through it the Vampyre renews its form. Those bitten by such beings seldom die outright. They weaken, pale, and in time, if taken thrice, become like the creature that damned them — enslaved to its will. Legends warn that a Vampyre may be undone only by purification through fire, the piercing of its heart with a consecrated stake, and the separation of head and body before the rising sun. Yet even then, the earth may remember its curse. It is said that if one listens closely in old crypts, faintly beneath the soil, they may still hear the heartbeat of those who refused to die.

Classification: Sentient Undead
Threat Level: High — lethal to solitary humans and small settlements
