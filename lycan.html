THE LYCANTHROPE

Where moonlight shines, the beast awakens.

January 17, 2001 Captain Rhys Godwin

The affliction known as Lycanthropy has haunted mankind since the dawn of superstition. Its nature lies at the border of disease and damnation — a curse passed through the mingling of blood, often from bite or claw. Victims appear ordinary by day, but under the full moon, a monstrous change overtakes them. Bones elongate, muscles distort, and the senses sharpen beyond mortal comprehension. The afflicted howl not merely in pain but in rapture, for the beast within is freed of conscience. Once transformed, the Lycanthrope loses all semblance of humanity, hunting the scent of fear with animal precision.

Those who survive such encounters describe eyes that gleam with intelligence, as if two souls war within one body — the man pleading, the beast devouring. At dawn, the creature reverts, left broken and stained with the horrors of the night. Ancient orders once sought to cure Lycanthropy through alchemical rites — silver-laced tonics, lunar bloodletting, or burning of the afflicted’s shadow. None succeeded. The curse endures, passed from victim to victim, until both memory and lineage are consumed by it.

Classification: Cursed Hybrid
Threat Level: Severe — highly contagious and lethal under lunar influence
