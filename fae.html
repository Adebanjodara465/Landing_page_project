THE FAE

Where beauty conceals peril, and promises bind the soul.

May 11, 1328 Archivist N. Morn

The Fae, known also as the Fair Folk, dwell between worlds — in forests untouched by iron and time. Their beauty is radiant yet unnatural, their laughter like the sound of breaking glass. They are neither good nor evil, but utterly other, moved by whims beyond human sense. Fae courts are ruled by queens older than civilization, whose crowns are woven from frost and madness. Gifts from them are deadly; refuse their kindness, and they curse you with eternal longing. Accept, and you belong to them — body, name, and soul.

Their glamour is a weapon. One look into their eyes can unmake the will. The wise traveller carries iron, bread, or salt — trifles to us, but poison to them. Do not speak your true name in their presence; do not eat their food, for one bite may bind you to their revels until the end of time. Even the wind that stirs their paths carries whispers of vanished children and kings who wandered into the woods and never returned.

Classification: Extramortal Entity — Realm of Twilight
Threat Level: Extreme — Metaphysical
